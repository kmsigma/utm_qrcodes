How to Use This Tool

Overview

This tool builds UTM-tagged campaign URLs and generates a scannable QR code from them — ready to embed in print materials, emails, or ads. All processing happens in your browser; nothing is sent to any server.

UTM Parameters

UTM parameters are small tags added to the end of a URL. Analytics platforms such as Google Analytics read them to tell you exactly where a visitor came from.

Website URL required
The destination page, e.g. https://example.com/landing. If you paste a URL that already contains UTM tags, they are automatically extracted and dropped into the fields below.
Source — utm_source required
The platform or referrer sending the visitor. Examples: google, linkedin, newsletter, event_badge.
Medium — utm_medium required
The marketing channel type. Examples: cpc, email, social, qr, print.
Campaign Name — utm_campaign
A label for the specific campaign or promotion. Examples: spring_sale, product_launch_2025.
Campaign ID — utm_id
A unique identifier for the campaign — useful when your ad platform assigns its own ID that you want to match in analytics.
Term — utm_term
The paid-search keyword that triggered the ad. Examples: running+shoes, cloud+security.
Content — utm_content
Distinguishes multiple links or creatives in A/B tests pointing to the same URL. Examples: banner_a, text_link, hero_cta.

QR Code & Actions

The QR code is regenerated automatically as you type. It encodes the fully assembled campaign URL at 512 × 512 pixels.

  • Copy URL — copies the final tagged URL to your clipboard.
  • Share — copies a link that opens this page with the form pre-filled. The center image (if any) is embedded in the link. No data is stored on a server.
  • Download — saves the QR code as a PNG file. The filename is derived from the medium and campaign name you entered.

Center Image

Upload a square PNG (max 5 MB) to overlay in the centre of the QR code — typically a logo. The image is automatically resized to 128 × 128 px on upload.

QR codes use built-in error correction, so a small centered image does not break scannability. Aim to keep the overlay under ~25% of the QR code area.

Saving & History

Campaigns are saved to your browser's localStorage — they persist between sessions on this device and browser, but are not synced to any other device or server. Up to 200 entries are kept.

  • Enter an optional Save name, then click the save icon to add the current configuration to your history.
  • Hover over a saved item to see a preview of its URL and UTM parameters.
  • Click the load icon to restore a saved campaign into the form.
  • Click the trash icon to delete a single entry, or use the bin icon in the column header to clear all history at once.

Tips

  • Use lowercase, underscores, and no spaces in UTM values to keep analytics data consistent — e.g. spring_sale not Spring Sale.
  • The utm_medium value qr is a common convention for QR-code campaigns.
  • Test your QR code with a physical device before printing.
  • Clearing browser data or switching browsers will remove your saved history.
